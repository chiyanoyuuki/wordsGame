<h2 class="main-title">Overview</h2>
<p><span><span><span><span><em><span>“Come here, come here. Ehehe, there is nothing to be afraid of. I won't break you. I would only play rough enough to break you if my Saint Graph was in a state where I am unaware of delights”</span></em></span></span></span></span></p>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>Descended from Mount Ibuki like a natural disaster, the avatar of the mythical serpent Yamata-no-Orochi - Ibuki-Douji is a Buster-centric Saber that can mow down legions of enemies with her otherworldly strength. Possessing a straightforward yet powerful skill set, Ibuki-Douji is a stellar pick even amongst the highly competitive selection of Saber Servants, and certainly one that most Masters should consider for their roster, be it for farming or challenge quests. </span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>Ibuki’s massive frame makes it quite obvious that her physical prowess is not to be trifled with. All three of Ibuki-Douji’s skills share the same characteristic of being an immense power boost to her already impressive natural high base Attack, a triple Buster cards deck and a selection of simple but great passives. </span></span></span></span></span></span><strong>Strength of Mountains and Rivers </strong><span><span><span><span><span><span>is both her NP Battery and main steroid, boosting her Attack for 3 hits within 3 turns, ensuring Ibuki has no problem accessing her Noble Phantasm and hitting hard at the same time. </span></span></span></span></span></span><strong>Octo-Pulsing Billow </strong><span><span><span><span><span><span>further amplifies her firepower through boosting her Buster Cards’ effectiveness for three turns, on top of an extra Defense buff and a 20 Critical Stars bomb. Finally, </span></span></span></span></span></span><strong>Fingertips of Defilement</strong> <span><span><span><span><span><span>rounds out Ibuki’s offense by granting her a strong Critical Damage boost and anti-Undead power mod, with a single target NP Seal debuff to a random enemy as the icing on the cake. Ibuki’s Noble Phantasm - the legendary sword Kusanagi - is one of the rare AoE Noble Phantasms in the game that will almost always hit for full damage, thanks to applying Ignore Invincible and Ignore Defense to Ibuki before dealing damage. Putting everything together, it is quite apparent that this Serpent Goddess is an offensive powerhouse that would excel in both wave clearing and single-target damage. While her effects and skills look rather simple, their potent value means both her face cards and Noble Phantasm hit exceedingly hard and consistently. </span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>But even a goddess can have imperfections. And one such is Ibuki’s lack of hard defense. While her natural bulk is respectable thanks to her Defense buff and Damage Cut passive, Ibuki does not have the means to avoid massive hits. Without teammates covering for her, this can pose a major problem in high difficulty content. Furthermore, outside of a single-turn Critical Star bomb, Ibuki lacks other means to continuously dish out high damage Critical Hits, making her inconsistent as a Critical Damage Dealer without external support. </span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>Ibuki-Douji is the epitome of the simplest form of gameplay in the game, and she is very effective at it. Her strengths are easy to utilize while also leaving ample room for a high performance ceiling through proper strategy and team building, and her weaknesses are not anything close to crippling. The Buster AoE Saber role has traditionally been an overcrowded niche, but Ibuki still easily stands out as a premier pick for any Master. </span></span></span></span></span></span></p>

<div id="servant-strengths">
<h3><span><span><span><strong><span><span>Immense Firepower</span></span></strong></span></span></span></h3>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>Thanks to her several different high-value steroids that last over multiple turns, fantastic base Attack, and triple Buster deck, Ibuki-Douji’s damage output is both impressive and consistent. Both her face cards and Noble Phantasm can deal massive damage even with just her buffs and scales very well with further amplification. Her Critical Damage potential is also not to be understated, with a powerful Critical Damage buff and on-demand Critical Star Bomb.  </span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
<h3><span><span><span><strong><span><span>Fantastic Wave-clear Potential</span></span></strong></span></span></span></h3>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>Packing a 50% NP Battery, Ibuki is in rare company as a top-tier wave clearer. In the right setup, Ibuki can utilize her Noble Phantasm several times consecutively. With Append Skill and the arrival of the long-awaited Koyanskaya of Light and Oberon, Ibuki becomes a reliable wave-clearer for any Lancer-related nodes. </span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
<h3><span><span><span><strong><span><span>Great Utility for Challenging Contents</span></span></strong></span></span></span></h3>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>Ibuki has some neat effects on her skills that might go unnoticed at first. NP Seal, Ignore Invincible, Ignore Defense, and Anti-Undead bonus damage are all valuable effects that may swing the tide of the battle in many difficult quests. </span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
</div>
<h2 class="main-title">Weaknesses</h2>
<div id="servant-weaknesses">
<h3><span><span><span><strong><span><span>Lack of Hard Defense</span></span></strong></span></span></span></h3>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>Ibuki-Douji is naturally bulky thanks to her high base HP, Damage Cut passive and Defense Buff, but these are not enough in the face of hard hitting bosses. For any long battle, it is highly advisable that she is deployed alongside teammates that can provide her other forms of protections such as Evasion or Invincibility. </span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
<h3><span><span><span><strong><span><span>Lack of Means to Generate Critical Stars Consistently</span></span></strong></span></span></span></h3>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>Outside of her Star Bomb on her second skill, Ibuki-Douji does not have any other reliable means to supply Critical Stars for her monstrous Buster cards. This may prove to be a headache to utilize her optimally, as sometimes the timing of her second skill will not coincide well with her cards being chosen, and over the course of any long battle, it is highly likely that her Critical Damage buffs might be wasted. </span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
</div>

<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>All of Ibuki-Douji's skills are incredible and should be leveled. It is highly recommended to max her first skill as soon as possible, as both of its effects are very potent and necessary for her to function. From then, her second skill will be a better option to prioritize as it improves her damage in general, while her third skill leans toward more utility and Critical niche. </span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
<ul><li><strong>Strength of Mountains and Rivers</strong><span><span><span><strong><span><span> A+ </span></span></strong><span><span><span>– Ibuki’s best skill without question. Raising it level improves both her NP Battery value and Attack buff, both of which are invaluable. </span></span></span></span></span></span></li>
<li><strong>Octo-Pulsing Billow B</strong><span><span><span><span><span><span> – This skill is Ibuki’s second steroid, which helps both her Noble Phantasm and face cards as she possesses 4 Buster cards in total. The number of Critical Stars from its Star Bomb effect and the Defense can also be very handy in various circumstances. </span></span></span></span></span></span></li>
<li><strong>Fingertips of Defilement</strong><span><span><span><strong><span><span> A </span></span></strong><span><span><span>– While strong, this skill holds less importance than her other skills. Its main value from leveling up is the Critical Damage steroid which may or may not come up depending on the team Ibuki-Douji is in, and while NP Seal and Damage against Undead are strong, they are much less widely applicable. </span></span></span></span></span></span></li>
</ul>

<li><span><span><span><strong><span><span>Black Grail / Heaven’s Feel: </span></span></strong></span></span></span><span><span><span><span><span><span>For any setup that wants to maximize her Noble Phantasm damage, then these Craft Essences are unparalleled. </span></span></span></span></span></span></li>

<h2 class="main-title">Profile</h2>
<div class="paragraph paragraph--type--servant-profile-entry paragraph--view-mode--default">
<h3 class="sub-title" style="margin-top:8px">Character Info</h3>
<p>Ibuki-Douji.</p>
<p>Another name for Shuten-Douji, specific to the Ibuki Mountains in the Omi region. Ibuki-Douji, born in most unnatural circumstances—being a child of oni—grew up in the mountains, eventually gaining frightful supernatural powers.</p>
<p>It is said that she migrated to Mt. Ooe and became the great monster Shuten-Douji, who gorged herself on blood and plunder in the capital...</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph paragraph--type--servant-profile-entry paragraph--view-mode--default">
<h3 class="sub-title" style="margin-top:8px">Profile 1</h3>
<p>Height/Weight:<br />
First Ascension: 133cm (Overall 200cm), ???kg<br />
Second and Third Ascension: 180cm (300cm during combat), ???kg<br />
Origin: Shuten-Douji Emaki, Otogizoushi, etc.<br />
Region: Japan<br />
Alignment: Chaotic-Good<br />
Gender: Female<br />
She is another aspect of Shuten-Douji, the anti-hero, as well as a Divine Spirit.</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph paragraph--type--servant-profile-entry paragraph--view-mode--default">
<h3 class="sub-title" style="margin-top:8px">Profile 2</h3>
<p>Her divine aspects are quite prominent, and she is very aware that she is part of nature. As such, she tends to act on her own primal urges. Accordingly, she has no intention of integrating or establishing relationships with the world of human culture and civilization. Though she may create the impression of a certain naivete, an ignorance of the pleasures of alcohol, a lack of experience with the finer luxuries of life, or even an innocence of the madness that so often takes people in the course of their lives, the moment she loses herself to her desires, she proves to be entirely unstoppable. So long as she is not provoked, she remains quite harmless, though she is at the same time far more dangerous than Shuten-Douji.</p>
<p>The moment she shows interest in something, she grows extremely attached. As long as the object of her affection continues to exist, she is prone to simply staring at it for extended periods. More often than not, when she so much as touches her obsession, she destroys it. At the very least, she brings such profound unhappiness to it as to end in the poor unfortunate's death. As her other aspect is an oni, she is especially drawn to other oni. Additionally, beings of the same types as her (children of divine dragons) tend to draw her keen interest as well.</p>
<p>◆</p>
<p>The above purports to be a description of Ibuki-Douji's nature, but the version of her which has manifested in Chaldea has proven to be a bit...different.</p>
<p>She has been...completely beguiled by the modern world.</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph paragraph--type--servant-profile-entry paragraph--view-mode--default">
<h3 class="sub-title" style="margin-top:8px">Profile 3</h3>
<p>Her divine nature makes it largely impossible for her to see the world in the same manner as humanity does, but she tends to present herself as a big sister of sorts, behaving as if she has perfectly acclimated to the modern world. She seems to have studied a great deal about what it means to be human. She may even try to tempt or provoke those around her, including Master. Sherlock Holmes believes she either studied modern society somewhere, or met a Heroic Spirit who had, like her, grown more acclimated to the modern world. He believes that she was then able to use Chaldea's unique summoning system to her advantage to somehow bring that information with her into this manifestation. The truth, however, remains a mystery.</p>
<p>The moment the True Name of her Noble Phantasm is revealed, however, she reverts immediately to her more divine aspect. Even so, she seems to tremendously enjoy her persona as a carefree "big sister snake."</p>
<p>◆</p>
<p>As a side note, when she is in her juvenile form (her First Ascension), her manner of speech and behavior are a mixture of her "big sister snake" and more divine personas. Her warnings about being "dangerous when she molts" likely refer to this more divine part of her aspect.</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph paragraph--type--servant-profile-entry paragraph--view-mode--default">
<h3 class="sub-title" style="margin-top:8px">Profile 4</h3>
<p>Core of the Serpent God: A<br />
A compound skill that includes Divinity. It should be inconceivable for her to possess Divinity since her legend says she is part human...but since Ibuki-Douji is also the child of Yamata-no-Orochi, she retains this skill at a high rank. Should she be reduced to an oni, she loses this skill, and it is replaced by a lower-ranked Divinity.</p>
<p>Octo-Pulsing Billow: B<br />
A skill that inherited the nature of the flood deity, one of the aspects of Yamata-no-Orochi. It represents Ibuki's unchanging physique which destroys all things with its raging aggression.</p>
<p>Fingertips of Defilement: A<br />
The loss of purity, a lack of honor, and finally death. Anything Ibuki-Douji touches is rendered impure, and may not exist in the presence of gods or humans. This skill may be categorized as an offensive skill, but it also grants additional effects against the dead, Demonic Beasts, and fallen gods even in conversation.</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph paragraph--type--servant-profile-entry paragraph--view-mode--default">
<h3 class="sub-title" style="margin-top:8px">Profile 5</h3>
<p>[Sword of Gods - Kusanagi-no-Tachi]<br />
Rank: A+<br />
NP Type: Anti-Army/Anti-Fortress<br />
Range: 1 - 60<br />
Maximum Targets: 500</p>
<p>Shinken - Kusanagi-no-Tachi.<br />
According to the "Kojiki" and "Nihon-shoki," a man called Susanoo-no-Mikoto, who was exiled from Takamagahara, came to the nation of Izumo where he saved a woman named Kusanadahime from being sacrificed, and killed a giant monster in the process. That monster was the eight-headed, eight-tailed, red-eyed creature Yamata-no-Orochi...a gigantic serpent whose body spanned over eight valleys and eight peaks. It was Japan's largest rampaging dragon.</p>
<p>Susanoo-no-Mikoto managed to defeat this giant serpent and obtained a divine sword from its tail. The sword was called Kusanagi-no-Tachi, also known as Kusanagi-no-Tsurugi, Ame-no-Murakumo-no-Tsurugi, and Tsumugari-no-Tachi. It was later kept at Atsuta Jingu as one of the three sacred treasures of Japan.</p>
<p>Ibuki-Douji wields this divine sword that was inside her father, Yamata-no-Orochi, as her Noble Phantasm. Upon its True Name release, the sword becomes a weapon so terrifying that, a single swing of it cuts open eight valleys and peaks and creates eight great rivers. However, according to Ibuki-Douji, the Noble Phantasm only wields a tiny fraction of the divine sword's brilliance, and temporarily at that.</p>
