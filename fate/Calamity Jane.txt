<h2 class="main-title">Overview</h2>
<p><span><span><span><span><em><span>Sure they call me Calamity, but thats really just because I get unlucky from time to time!</span></em></span></span></span></span></p>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>Calamity Jane falls into the category of SR Servants who pair a mix of party support tools with their own offensive abilities. These Servants tend to lean towards Quick, and Jane is no exception. With an ST Quick NP and a suite of useful (albeit limited) buffs, Jane comes ready to plug into a Quick team and help it on its way. She has a set of meaningful weaknesses that keep her from really excelling in her role, but in cases where her specific utility is needed, she can truly shine.</span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>All three of Calamity Janes skills are supportive in nature. </span></span></span></span></span></span><span><span><span><strong><span><span>Sabotage </span></span></strong></span></span></span><span><span><span><span><span><span>and </span></span></span></span></span></span><span><span><span><strong><span><span>Messenger of the Galaxy </span></span></strong></span></span></span><span><span><span><span><span><span>both provide modest-but-useful benefits, keeping enemies under control and boosting the partys overall performance. </span></span></span></span></span></span><span><span><span><strong><span><span>O Polar Star, Shine My Way </span></span></strong></span></span></span><span><span><span><span><span><span>is a bit more unusual. While it also supports an ally, its effects depend on the number of critical stars available. With enough critical stars, Jane has access to a set of powerful utility effects, with the skills three-turn Ignore Invincible effect being particularly useful for some difficult fights. Janes Noble Phantasm, </span></span></span></span></span></span><span><span><span><strong><span><span>Space Dead Mans Hand</span></span></strong></span></span></span><span><span><span><span><span><span>, also has the ability to output damage in a pinch, due to its ST Quick typing.</span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>Janes various strengths come paired with some serious drawbacks. Most notably among them, Janes supportive value is considerably lessened if she does not have critical stars available. Her party damage buffs are also limited in various ways, from having a fail chance, to being tied to her Noble Phantasm, to having a short duration. Together these mean Jane is unlikely to be an appealing support except in cases where her specific combination of utility is needed. Additionally, despite Janes ability to deal damage, her low selfish damage buffs mean her actual damage output will be low, making her a weak pick for a primary damage-dealer.</span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>Jane is not a universally useful Servant. Her value is mostly limited to difficult fights, and she requires access to critical stars in order to be maximally effective. As such, she needs specific teams and specific fights in order to really shine. In cases where she can make good use of her specialized utility, though, she can absolutely be helpful to have on-hand.</span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
<h2 class="main-title">Strengths</h2>
<div id="servant-strengths">
<h3><span><span><span><span><span><span>Valuable Utility</span></span></span></span></span></span></h3>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>With 50 critical stars, Jane brings a range of useful utility effects to the team. She can charge one allys NP gauge by 30%, which is rare and useful in itself, and she also gives that Servant Ignore Invincible status for three turns. This can help that ally work more reliably in hard fights with many defensive skills. Jane also brings other generally-useful effects, enabling critical attacks and protecting the party from enemy criticals, which helps the team move faster and stay alive longer.</span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
<h3><span><span><span><span><span><span>Party Damage Buffs</span></span></span></span></span></span></h3>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>Jane can increase the entire teams attack, and her Noble Phantasm reduces a targets Defense and Quick Resistance. With these effects together, Jane can help support an allied damage-dealers Noble Phantasm, while also boosting her own damage (or the damage of another semi-support) in the process.</span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
<h3><span><span><span><span><span><span>Strong NP Gain</span></span></span></span></span></span></h3>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>Jane has unusually high Arts card NP gain for a Servant with two Arts cards. She can reliably use her Noble Phantasm repeatedly, especially with the help of her own NP charge skills.</span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
</div>
<h2 class="main-title">Weaknesses</h2>
<div id="servant-weaknesses">
<h3><span><span><span><span><span><span>Critical Star Requirements</span></span></span></span></span></span></h3>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>While Jane brings a range of useful utility when the team has 50 critical stars, her value diminishes with fewer stars available. In order to guarantee Jane will be maximally effective, masters must bring Starting Critical Stars craft essences or Servants with Critical Star Gain skills to fuel Janes third skill. This limits the teams in which Jane works well and requires having specific Servants or Craft Essences on hand.</span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
<h3><span><span><span><span><span><span>Damage Boost Restrictions</span></span></span></span></span></span></h3>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>Each of Janes damage boosts is limited in some way. The ally attack boost on Messenger of the Galaxy can miss. Furthermore, Janes Defense and Quick Resistance debuffs are tied to her Noble Phantasm, requiring NP charge skills or CEs in order to access them. These debuffs also only last two turns, rather than the standard three, making them less effective than typical supportive effects of their type.</span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
<h3><span><span><span><span><span><span>Low Damage Output</span></span></span></span></span></span></h3>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>Due to her supportive focus, Jane has significantly lower self damage buffs than a typical Single-Target Servant. As such, while she can access her Noble Phantasm fairly easily, her actual damage output will be relatively low, making her a weak dedicated damage-dealer.</span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
</div>

At max level, this skill will reliably prevent critical attacks from enemies with low critical attack rates, which can be useful, but aside from that this skill doesnt do much.</span></span></span></span></span></span></li>

It drains the entire enemy teams NP gauge, helping to delay enemy NP turns. At the same time, its party NP charge effect can enable ally NPs. The skill also increases Janes own attack and has a chance to increase ally attack, making this skill a useful damage buff when Jane is on the field.</span></span></span></span></span></span></li>
<li><span><span><span><strong><span><span>O Polar Star, Shine My Way </span></span></strong><span><span><span>grows in value depending on the number of Critical Stars the party has. With no Critical Stars, all this skill is useful for is guaranteeing Janes own attack boost succeeds (or helping an ally with Imperial Privilege or a similar skill with a fail chance). With 10 critical stars, the skill gains a critical damage buff, although the team will likely need more than 10 critical stars in order to actually use that buff. With 20 stars, Jane helps an ally draw stars to crit. With 30 stars, this skill gains an Evade buff, giving it some defensive value. With 40 stars, it gains a three-turn Ignore Invincible buff, which can be important for reliably clearing some difficult fights. At 50 stars, the skill picks up a 20% NP charge effect, maximizing Janes supportive value. Masters will need to keep in mind how many stars are required for each of Janes effects when deciding when and how to use this skill. It should be noted that the skill does not consume critical stars upon use, unlike some other skills that have star requirements, and can be used even without any stars (albeit without effects that require more stars).</span></span></span></span></span></span></li>

<h2 class="main-title">Profile</h2>
<div class="paragraph paragraph--type--servant-profile-entry paragraph--view-mode--default">
<h3 class="sub-title" style="margin-top:8px">Character Info</h3>
<p>A happy-go-lucky spacecheerleader who marches to the beat of her own drum, hailing from the Servant Universe. She loves anything new, and she never sweats the small stuff. How little does she care? Well, so little so that she tends to forget the grudges or debts made even a day ago.</p>
<p>"Huh? You dropped your phone in that crevasse over there? Leave it to me! Let's start with explosives"</p>
<p>Now that's what you call upbeat. Despite her questionable behavior, she was said to be one of the galaxy's best scouts back in the day.</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph paragraph--type--servant-profile-entry paragraph--view-mode--default">
<h3 class="sub-title" style="margin-top:8px">Profile 1</h3>
<p>Height/Weight: 168cm, 60kg<br />
Origin: History<br />
Region: The Servant Universe<br />
Alignment: Chaotic-Balanced<br />
Gender: Female<br />
"I'm a little fuzzy on the details, but I guess my True Name in this universe is supposed to be Martha Jane Cannary! Whoo, that me sounds really cool!"</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph paragraph--type--servant-profile-entry paragraph--view-mode--default">
<h3 class="sub-title" style="margin-top:8px">Profile 2</h3>
<p>A scout and gunman from America's frontier days.<br />
While technically not an outlaw, her supposed accomplishments were either exaggerated or of dubious merit, and in her life she was both praised and criticized. Her uncertain background is the reason why most of her skills are debuff types, despite her optimistic personality.</p>
<p>Her name itself means disaster, and she lived through the same period as Billy the Kid during America's Wild West. She was an expert gunman, a brilliant scout, the lover of Wild Bill Hickok, who was called the "Champion of the Plains," and as she and Hickok fought through countless deadly campaigns, she herself was given the nickname "Queen of the Plains."</p>
<p>There are two theories about how she became known as Calamity. The first is based on the legend of her being a savior from calamity, in which it is said that she saved a group of cavalrymen from an ambush by Native Americans. The other is based on her being called the "woman who brings forth calamity," which purports that all her lovers were killed in battle. Some also suggest both are true.</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph paragraph--type--servant-profile-entry paragraph--view-mode--default">
<h3 class="sub-title" style="margin-top:8px">Profile 3</h3>
<p>She is the life of the party, always cheering for others rather than elevating herself.<br />
Her extreme optimism enables her to always believe there is a future. Even if the existence of space itself is at stake, she never loses hope, and she will stubbornly believe in its survival.<br />
While her intentions are all good, the actions she takes really make one question her aims. She's the kind of person who would demolish a house to get needed building materials for a bridge on the brink of collapse.</p>
<p>She is never critical of others, preferring to sing the praises of their accomplishments, big or small. This is why she gets along with Space Ishtar, who often gets carried away...er, is someone who works hard after a bit of encouragement. She is a competent space scout, and her survival skills are second to none.<br />
However, she has zero life skills.<br />
Against targets not within her line of sight, she has high spec sabotage and disruption capacity, but her combat abilities drastically decrease against direct targets.</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph paragraph--type--servant-profile-entry paragraph--view-mode--default">
<h3 class="sub-title" style="margin-top:8px">Profile 4</h3>
<p>[Space Dead Man's Hand]<br />
Rank: D<br />
NP Type: Anti-Personnel<br />
Range: 1 - 50<br />
Maximum Target: 1</p>
<p>The Black Hand That Invites Death.<br />
"Bad Luck" is engraved on a bullet and shot toward the back of a target's head.<br />
Even if the shot is fired at point-blank range, the bullet will always loop around to the target's back.<br />
This special attack is said to induce a vision of a pair black aces and black eights, the same way her lover and comrade, the Champion of the Plains, Wild Bill Hickok, died.<br />
According to history, Wild Bill was playing a hand of poker at a saloon in Deadwood, South Dakota, when he was shot and killed by Jack McCall from behind. At the time of his assassination, Wild Bill was said to be holding a two-pair hand of black aces and eights, which has since become widely known as the "dead man's hand."</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph paragraph--type--servant-profile-entry paragraph--view-mode--default">
<h3 class="sub-title" style="margin-top:8px">Profile 5</h3>
<p>Sabotage: B<br />
A capable scout's skill.<br />
The ability to find blind spots and weaknesses in an enemy's attack and positioning, reducing their combat effectiveness. The same as Fuuma Kotarou's Sabotage skill.</p>
<p>Messenger of the Galaxy: EX<br />
A skill based on the story that she crossed a frigid river in order to deliver important dispatches.<br />
It decreases the enemy's morale while increasing her allies' morale.<br />
However, because the validity of her tale is uncertain, it's probability-based when applied to allies. (Since she believes in herself, Jane herself will power up without fail.)</p>
<p>O Polar Star, Shine My Way: B<br />
The minimized version of her Noble Phantasm that she no longer uses.<br />
It's a unique skill where its effect proportionally increases based on the amount of Critical Stars available.<br />
It's a Noble Phantasm that originally carried a single ray of hope intended for a comeback against next-dimensional threats that humanity cannot possibly beat on its own.</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph paragraph--type--servant-profile-entry paragraph--view-mode--default">
<h3 class="sub-title" style="margin-top:8px">Profile 6</h3>
<p>[Sentinel Stellaris]<br />
Rank: A<br />
NP Type: Anti-Goddess<br />
Range: 1 - 99<br />
Maximum Target: 1</p>
<p>O Polar Star, Illuminate My Enemy.<br />
Jane's second Noble Phantasm.<br />
One of the Noble Phantasms called the "Four Cards" used by the squadron that beat the primordial goddess.<br />
The enemy's weakness is detected, and its state gets fixed as the "truth."<br />
By defeating the primordial goddess, Jane was inflicted with a curse, causing her to no longer be able to utilize this Noble Phantasm.</p>
<p></p>
<p>In the Servant Universe 2000 years ago, Jane joined the team that took on the primordial goddess, serving as their scout. While the mission was a success thanks to the accurate intel she gathered, she received a curse of immortality after killing the goddess, causing her to no longer be capable of dying until the goddess reawakened.<br />
A typical person would be driven mad by such endless wandering, but Jane's innate cheerfulness has enabled her to endure the curse.</p>
</div>

  function getRank(array, stat){
    var rank = -1;
    var count = 0;
    for(var i = 0; i < array.length; i++){
      if(array[i] == stat){
        rank = i+1;
        return rank;
      }
    }
  }
  function getInfo(array, field){
    var uniques = [];
    var highest = -1;
    for(var i = 0; i < array.length; i++){
      var currVal = array[i][field];
      var currValInt = parseInt(currVal);
      var currClass = array[i].tdoll_class;
      if(currValInt > highest){
        highest = currValInt;
      }
      uniques.push(currVal);
    }
    return [uniques, highest];
  }

function sortJSONByField(json, field){
  return json.concat().sort(function(a, b){
    return parseInt(b[field]) - parseInt(a[field]);
  });
}
