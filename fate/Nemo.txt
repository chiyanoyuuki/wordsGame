<h2 class="main-title">Overview</h2>
<p><span><span><span><span><em><span>“Are you used to the Nautilus yet?”</span></em></span></span></span></span></p>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>The captain of the Nautilus finally makes himself summonable with the arrival of the Imaginary Scramble event. As a single-target Arts Rider, Nemo specializes in taking down certain specific targets. Fitting his role as captain of a ship, his power varies substantially depending on where and what he’s fighting. He won’t always shine, but in his favored terrain, he can be very powerful indeed.</span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>Nemo gets a number of damage bonuses from field and enemy types, meaning </span></span></span></span></span></span><span><span><span><strong><span><span>Great Ram Nautilus</span></span></strong></span></span></span><span><span><span><span><span><span>, his Noble Phantasm, can potentially deal a lot of damage to a single enemy. The buffs on </span></span></span></span></span></span><span><span><span><strong><span><span>Voyager of the Storm</span></span></strong></span></span></span><span><span><span><span><span><span> and </span></span></span></span></span></span><span><span><span><strong><span><span>Guide for Travel </span></span></strong></span></span></span><span><span><span><span><span><span>also apply to Nemo’s allies, letting him increase the whole party’s damage output. Furthermore, Nemo has strong NP gain, along with multiple NP charge effects on </span></span></span></span></span></span><span><span><span><strong><span><span>Indefatigable</span></span></strong></span></span></span><span><span><span><span><span><span>, making him good at repeatedly using his Noble Phantasm.</span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>Unfortunately, many of Nemo’s buffs are dependent on being on a Near Water or Imaginary Space field, meaning his damage and his support drop significantly outside of those situations. Nemo also has no defensive tools other than a single Guts effect, so he often needs protection to stay alive in hard fights. Additionally, despite being a Rider with relatively easy critical attack access, Nemo has few buffs that apply to critical hits and other normal attacks, leaving his normal attack damage fairly low.</span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>Nemo is very situational. In fights where Nemo benefits from all of his specialty buffs, he is a very powerful single-target Servant. He also has value in cases where having two damage-dealers is useful, as Nemo can share his buffs with his allies. When Nemo does not get to use his field-based buffs, though, his performance is rather lackluster.</span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
<h2 class="main-title">Strengths</h2>
<div id="servant-strengths">
<h3><span><span><span><span><span><span>High Damage Potential</span></span></span></span></span></span></h3>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>On Near Water and Imaginary Space fields, Nemo gets an extra 20% attack, Arts Card effectiveness, and NP damage up, letting him deal substantially increased damage with his Noble Phantasm. He also deals supereffective damage to Super Giant enemies. When Nemo gets both of these benefits, he is an excellent single-target damage-dealer.</span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
<h3><span><span><span><span><span><span>Party Damage Support</span></span></span></span></span></span></h3>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>Many of Nemo’s buffs apply to the entire party. Especially on Near Water or Imaginary Space fields, Nemo provides an unusual amount of damage support for a damage-dealer. This makes him a good choice for teams that need two damage-dealers, provided the fight is on one of Nemo’s favored fields.</span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
<h3><span><span><span><span><span><span>Strong NP Gain</span></span></span></span></span></span></h3>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>Nemo has very good Arts card NP gain, and Indefatigable provides a flat NP charge. Together, these make it easy for Nemo to use his NP repeatedly. Indefatigable also gives an additional 50% NP charge when its Guts effect activates, letting Nemo use his NP more easily in fights where he takes a lot of damage.</span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
</div>
<h2 class="main-title">Weaknesses</h2>
<div id="servant-weaknesses">
<h3><span><span><span><span><span><span>Highly Situational Power Levels</span></span></span></span></span></span></h3>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>While Nemo’s NP damage is very high in his favored situations, it is also very low when those situations do not apply. Similarly, Nemo’s support value is substantially reduced outside of his favored terrain. This means Nemo is unlikely to be a good choice as a damage-dealer unless the fight allows him to benefit from his niche bonuses.</span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
<h3><span><span><span><span><span><span>Limited Defensive Tools</span></span></span></span></span></span></h3>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>Indefatigable is Nemo’s only defensive tool, and it is on a long cooldown. As such, Nemo typically needs the help of defensive support Servants in order to stay alive in difficult or long fights.</span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
<h3><span><span><span><span><span><span>Weak Normal Attacks</span></span></span></span></span></span></h3>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>Nemo has few critical attack damage buffs, and especially outside of his favored fields types, he also has few buffs that apply to his normal attacks in general. Most of his damage is concentrated on his Noble Phantasm, which may make it difficult for him to deal damage to enemies other than his primary target, or to output good damage when not looping his Noble Phantasm.</span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
</div>

<h2 class="main-title">Profile</h2>
<div class="paragraph paragraph--type--servant-profile-entry paragraph--view-mode--default">
<h3 class="sub-title" style="margin-top:8px">Character Info</h3>
<p>Triton, the merman who was raised and loved by the gods of the sea, and Prince Dakkar, the character in the novel "Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Seas," or at least the man he was probably based on. The combination of these two creates the Nemo we have here.</p>
<p>Although Phantom Spirits have only ever existed in the Pseudo-Singularity Shinjuku so far, an alchemist from Atlas named Sion managed to extract the data and summon this special Servant as a Phantom Spirit.</p>
<p>In Novum Chaldea, Nemo dispatches and supports units deployed in field operations. Operations on land fall under the jurisdiction of Da Vinci, while maritime operations are under Captain Nemo's authority, so it may not be an exaggeration to say that these two are rivals in that sense as well.</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph paragraph--type--servant-profile-entry paragraph--view-mode--default">
<h3 class="sub-title" style="margin-top:8px">Profile 1</h3>
<p>Height/Weight: 150cm, 40kg<br />
Origin: Greek Mythology, "Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Seas"<br />
Region: Europe, India<br />
Alignment: Chaotic-Balanced<br />
Gender: Unknown → Male<br />
Nemo's gender was initially unknown, as they were both Triton and a prince, but after adventuring with Chaldea, he decided to be "Captain Nemo" and took on a male form.</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph paragraph--type--servant-profile-entry paragraph--view-mode--default">
<h3 class="sub-title" style="margin-top:8px">Profile 2</h3>
<p>Triton was beloved by all for his honesty and kindheartedness. Prince Dakkar was known to be both decisive in his actions and judgment as well as a man of faith. As Nemo is a Phantom Spirit who combines these two Heroic Spirits, his personality slightly deviates from each of the originals.</p>
<p>As both Triton and Nemo, this Heroic Spirit gained his own unique ideal: rebellion against domination and devastation. Even if it be a god, Nemo will not tolerate those who abuse the weak. Ironically, this principle extends to him rebelling against modern human society (consumerism) as well. Even now, he tends to get irritated whenever he considers European culture. Of course, Greece wouldn't be an exception either, but according to him, "there are no national borders in the world of the ocean."</p>
<p>Upon his first appearance, Nemo did not show any emotion even when interacting with Protagonist or Mash. But after regaining the memories of Prince Dakkar, he developed an instinctual wariness for humans who are the authors of their own tragedies. So he can be very blunt at times...</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph paragraph--type--servant-profile-entry paragraph--view-mode--default">
<h3 class="sub-title" style="margin-top:8px">Profile 3</h3>
<p>As Nemo is in charge of the ship, he is tough on both himself and those around him, and he has little patience for indulgent behaviors, idleness, or selfishness. He is the consummate captain—tough as nails and possessed of a militaristic bearing. At the same time, as he is also Triton, he gives his blessing to all manner of adventurous journeys. Upon his summoning as a Servant in Chaldea, he deems Protagonist a travelling adventurer, and so offers his blessings and guidance.</p>
<p>At first, he adheres rigidly to his given role, hiding his true self and insisting that he is "just a submarine" even in his interactions with Master. Gradually, as he lets down his guard on recognizing his Master is trustworthy, he becomes much more companionable, even friendly. Naturally straightforward and goodhearted, he will never betray those to whom he's taken a liking.</p>
<p>Even if he cannot see the end of his friend's travels, he numbers among the Heroic Spirits who truly loves mankind, and so will always offer his blessings to humanity.</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph paragraph--type--servant-profile-entry paragraph--view-mode--default">
<h3 class="sub-title" style="margin-top:8px">Profile 4</h3>
<p>[Great Ram, Nautilus]<br />
Rank: A<br />
NP Type: Anti-Sea Noble Phantasm<br />
Range: 2 - 70<br />
Maximum Targets: 1 enemy</p>
<p>I Shall Conquer, the Great Ram of the Nautilus.<br />
Taking the shape of the submarine Nautilus, this Noble Phantasm surges forward with its giant ram. Endowed with a special concept, it may pierce any enemy, no matter how massive, be it a giant squid or a battleship. While this Noble Phantasm can be used outside the water (both above ground or in the air), it is far more accurate and powerful if it is used in the sea, making it more effective whenever the field is Near Water.</p>
<p>Voyager of the Storm: C++<br />
A skill employed by captains. This skill is a lower rank than many other captain Servants because Triton was technically not the captain of a ship, and Nemo's ship was an advanced technological marvel of his age, so he never had to sail through a howling gale on a tattered vessel. However, if the field is Near Water, this skill becomes vastly more efficient, which explains why its rank is appended with a "++".</p>
<p>Indefatigable: B+<br />
The spiritual nature and conviction of Nemo given form. While it's both a Battle Continuation and NP Gain skill, its true potential is demonstrated when Guts is activated, as the captain's spirit burns brightest in the direst of circumstances.</p>
<p>Guide for Travel: C++<br />
Triton, who once guided the Argo, is known for having been a guide of heroes. As Triton protects the hopes and dreams of those who seek uncharted territory and unknown lands, he is comparable with the young Da Vinci, whose fundamental drive is to go beyond in pursuit of a better world.<br />
This skill's effects also improve if the field is Near Water.</p>

<h3 class="sub-title" style="margin-top:8px">Profile 5</h3>
<p>Operating a submarine, much less a warship, would be impossible for even Nemo to do alone. Knowing Nemo’s hands would be tied, Sion applied one of the Atlas Intuition’s secret techniques known as “memory partition” at the time of Nemo’s summoning. As a result, by dispersing his own magical energy, Nemo is able to create clones that are essentially the same as him but specialized for the various roles needed for the operation of a submarine. The following are the Nemo Series that work in the docks.</p>
<p>Nemo Marine<br />
The most nimble of the series, there are always twelve of these (twelve active on duty, maximum twenty-four). They all have the same personality and appearance. Nemo Marines represent the labor force that cleans, operates, maintains, and performs other miscellaneous tasks around the ship. They are always cheerful and never hate anyone (though they may not like everyone). While they may scream and shout a bit in emergencies, they will always do their very best to resolve the problem. The Marines are also the members of the Nemo Series in whom Triton's aspects are most clearly expressed.</p>
<p>Nemo Professor<br />
Professor addresses everyone respectfully, which won Blackbeard’s friendship. As the name suggests, Professor works as the ship's resident intellectual and strategist, and is in charge of analysis, calculation, and devising strategies.</p>
<p>Nemo Engineer<br />
The stubborn chief engineer who runs the ship's engine room with a fierce workman's spirit. Hers is one of the most dangerous and demanding jobs on the ship and she is driven by her motherly love and belief that protecting humans is her duty. She also sees herself and Captain as equally ranked, and so thinks she can take his place if he ever screws up. She loves humanity a great deal, but loves the Nautilus just as much.</p>
<p>Nemo Nurse<br />
She is in charge of the infirmary and is the most mature, as well as tolerant and rational, of all the Nemo Series. Nurse always performs her duties with a cool head in an emergency.</p>
<p>Nemo Baker<br />
Baker is the member of the Nemo Series in charge of the most important part of the ship: the galley (bakery). Meals boost a soldier’s morale, so ultimately this is the most powerful Nemo.</p>

<h3 class="sub-title" style="margin-top:8px">Profile 6</h3>
<p><em>Unlocks after clearing Lostbelt 5-2: Olympus at Bond Lv.5</em></p>
<p>Sion summoned Nemo as a Phantom Spirit because she didn't use Chaldea's summoning system. She was unsure if the summoning would succeed, and so by combining two Heroic Spirits' Spirit Origins, she reinforced a Spirit Origin capable of becoming a Servant. As a result, Triton took the name and memories of Nemo, and became one with the "Noble Phantasm of the sea", the submarine Nautilus, that was associated with Nemo.</p>
<p>When Sion summoned him at the start of Part 2, he was still both a mermaid and a prince. But after joining Chaldea and deciding to help them out, he sealed up his mermaid self and fully embraced his Captain Nemo side in the name of conquering Atlantis. He later became the captain of the dimension-piercing battleship Storm Border and assisted in the battle for Novum Chaldea.</p>
<p>He LOVES parfaits.<br />

  function getRank(array, stat){
    var rank = -1;
    var count = 0;
    for(var i = 0; i < array.length; i++){
      if(array[i] == stat){
        rank = i+1;
        return rank;
      }
    }
  }
  function getInfo(array, field){
    var uniques = [];
    var highest = -1;
    for(var i = 0; i < array.length; i++){
      var currVal = array[i][field];
      var currValInt = parseInt(currVal);
      var currClass = array[i].tdoll_class;
      if(currValInt > highest){
        highest = currValInt;
      }
      uniques.push(currVal);
    }
    return [uniques, highest];
  }

function sortJSONByField(json, field){
  return json.concat().sort(function(a, b){
    return parseInt(b[field]) - parseInt(a[field]);
  });
}
