<h2 class="main-title">Overview</h2>
<p dir="ltr"><em>“Get Crushed!”</em></p>
<p dir="ltr"><span>Chopping away at anything that may get in his way, Paul Bunyan rides in on his giant blue ox named… wait. Paul Bunyan is a girl? That little ball is an ox? Originally a joke Servant created by Riyo (author of the Learning with Manga), Paul Bunyan is a budget Buster AoE NP servant with support ideal for all who wood chance upon her. </span></p>
<p dir="ltr"><span>Bunyan carries her weight well for a budget Berserker. She supports and roots on her team members with Buster and Critical Damage buffs via </span><strong>Delightful Comrades</strong><span>, healing via </span><strong>Bean Soup Lake</strong><span>, and an AoE defense down on </span><strong>Popcorn Blizzard</strong><span> and her NP <strong>Marvelous </strong></span><strong>Exploits</strong><span>. Her other main benefits come from her NP, which not only hits very decently for a 1-star Berserker, but also is the fastest NP in the game, chopping down her enemies in only 2-3 seconds on 2x speed. </span></p>
<p dir="ltr"><span>While Bunyan is great with damage, she often leaves in a hurry. With the lowest HP in the game and Berserker type disadvantage, she has next to no survivability to tap into, leafing her easily felled. She also does knot have good sustained performance as her longer cooldowns, low survivability, and single Arts card means she will likely only rev up her NP once. </span></p>
<p dir="ltr"><span>Paul Bunyan is a quick and easy servant to sap through an enemy wave in as little time as possible instead of lumbering around. Masters looking for a servant to speed-run farming quests, a low-cost Buster support, or a Servant to fire their NP, give buffs, and die, will be quite pleased to be able to stick her into their party. She walnut disappoint. </span></p>
<h2 class="main-title">Strengths</h2>
<div id="servant-strengths">
<h3 dir="ltr">Strong and Fast NP</h3>
<p dir="ltr"><span>At 2-3 seconds on 2x speed and an AoE Berserker NP, Bunyan is one of the best servants in the game to clear a wave in as little time as possible. Thanks to the plethora of buffs (48.8% bonus Buster damage at most) she possesses as well as the ease in getting her to NP5, her NPs pack quite a punch as well.</span></p>
<h3 dir="ltr">Good General Support</h3>
<p dir="ltr"><span>Bunyan packs a variety of offensive buffs for her companions, boosting their Buster skills, Crit Damage, and lowering enemy Defense for 3 turns (5 turns on her NP), making her one of the best budget Buster Supports available. In a farming team, the Buster Crit buffs will continue to carry over, potentially allowing for massive Criticals from her friends. And just for fun, she can heal her friends too if they get hurt. </span></p>
</div>
<h2 class="main-title">Weaknesses</h2>
<div id="servant-weaknesses">
<h3 dir="ltr">Low Survivability</h3>
<p dir="ltr"><span>As big as Bunyan is, she just can’t take a punch. She has the lowest HP in the game and Berserker type disadvantage, so an average attack may well take out over half her total HP. Without any strong defensive options available, she will struggle to stay alive even with external support. Some Masters may be able to use this weakness as a strength though by intentionally letting her fall to bring in a new Servant.</span></p>
<h3 dir="ltr">Weak Long-Term Performance</h3>
<p dir="ltr"><span>While Bunyan is great as a charge-and-fire servant, her contribution is much diminished after firing off her skills and NP. With only a single Arts card, she will not generate much NP on her own to potentially fire off a second NP. Along with a more steep 6-8 turn cooldown on her two most important skills, combined with her weak survivability, she will likely only be able to use her skills once.</span></p>
</div>

<p dir="ltr"><span>Bunyan’s skills are a boon for her entire party. As an offensive support Servant, Bunyan profits most from boosting the damage on her NP and those of her allies. As such, a Master should level Delightful Comrades first as the buffs carry over to multiple waves, then Popcorn Blizzard for more damage, and Bean Soup Lake last. Most skills can be safely left at level 6 unless a Master is highly in need of a Buster Support or a low budget enthusiast.</span></p>
<ul><li dir="ltr">
<p dir="ltr"><strong>Delightful Comrades A </strong><span>is Bunyan’s primary support, boosting both Buster and Critical Damage for the entire party for 3 turns. As this skill lasts a full 3 turns, Masters do not necessarily have to save this skill until Bunyan’s NP turn. Level this first. </span></p>
</li>
<li dir="ltr">
<p dir="ltr"><strong>Bean Soup Lake A</strong><span> is Bunyan’s partywide heal, giving the party up to 2000 HP at max level. While useful in theory, if the party takes sustained damage then Bunyan will most likely by dead. Level this last.</span></p>
</li>
<li dir="ltr">
<p dir="ltr"><strong>Popcorn Blizzard B</strong><span> acts as a defense down on all enemies for 3 turns, boosting the party’s damage. While it also lowers their healing received, most enemies do not have a notable heal, making this a niche application at best. Since this skill will only apply to the current wave, level it second.</span></p>
</li>
</ul>

<p dir="ltr"><span>As Bunyan’s main usage is to buff and fire off an NP fast, getting a </span><strong>Starting NP Gauge</strong><span> or hybridCE will help her the most. When one is not available, Masters could look towards using </span><strong>Attack Up/Taunt</strong><span> CEs to increase her general damage and/or get her to die more quickly for a free swap. She also can benefit from a </span><strong>Death Effect</strong><span> CE for when she inevitably dies to better support her party. </span></p>
<ul><li dir="ltr">
<p dir="ltr"><strong>Kaleidoscope / Imaginary Element / Dragon's Meridian:</strong><span> Bunyan’s primary purpose is to NP, and any CE that helps her NP earlier is always a good choice.</span></p>
</li>
<li dir="ltr">
<p dir="ltr"><strong>Holy Night Supper / Golden Sumo / First Sunrise / Partake with the King: </strong><span>If support is available to charge the rest of Bunyan’s NP gauge, these CEs will help her both NP earlier and deal more damage. </span></p>
</li>
<li dir="ltr">
<p dir="ltr"><strong>Gudaguda Poster Girl: </strong><span>If one can charge Bunyan’s NP bar to full with Servants and MCs, this CE’s target focus will help Bunyan die as a free swap, leaving Bunyan’s buffs behind. </span></p>
</li>
<li><strong>The Merciless One / 500 Year Obsession / Battle of Camlann: </strong><span>Bunyan is quite likely to die, so a death effect CE will often provide an additional support to the party. </span></li>

<h2 class="main-title">Profile</h2>
<div class="paragraph paragraph--type--servant-profile-entry paragraph--view-mode--default">
<h3 class="sub-title" style="margin-top:8px">Character Info</h3>
<p>A lumberjack who appears in tall tales originating in the American pioneering days. She is said to be a giant whose head brushes the very clouds.<br />
A number of legends chronicle Bunyan's many exploits, often claiming that it was her actions that created many of North America's distinct geographical features.<br />
No one's really sure why she was summoned as a young girl.</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph paragraph--type--servant-profile-entry paragraph--view-mode--default">
<h3 class="sub-title" style="margin-top:8px">Profile 1</h3>
<p>Height/Weight: Undetermined<br />
Origin: Legends<br />
Region: America, Canada<br />
Alignment: Neutral-Balanced<br />
Gender: Female<br />
It's plain to see that her usual height is well over three meters, but every time an attempt is made at an official measurement, her height differs, and so nothing has yet been recorded.</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph paragraph--type--servant-profile-entry paragraph--view-mode--default">
<h3 class="sub-title" style="margin-top:8px">Profile 2</h3>
<p>As in her legend, she is accompanied by a giant blue ox everywhere she goes. However, it looks much more like a stuffed animal than an ox...<br />
Rather, it may be one of those fearsome critters rumored to have existed during America's pioneering days, or maybe even be what is called a cryptid in present times.</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph paragraph--type--servant-profile-entry paragraph--view-mode--default">
<h3 class="sub-title" style="margin-top:8px">Profile 3</h3>
<p>Bunyan loves people, and so she continues to discover, expand, and develop uncharted lands because she believes that the continued progress of civilization truly leads to peoples' happiness.<br />
She will not give a second thought to destroying great swaths of nature if it means the people can live wealthy, comfortable lives.<br />
How her actions will affect humanity in the future...the young Bunyan has no way of knowing at this point.</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph paragraph--type--servant-profile-entry paragraph--view-mode--default">
<h3 class="sub-title" style="margin-top:8px">Profile 4</h3>
<p>[Marvelous Exploits]<br />
Rank: A<br />
NP Type: Anti-Army<br />
Astounding Achievements.<br />
Using the United States of America itself as a conceptual Noble Phantasm, the country's founding history is converted into energy and slammed into the enemy with titanic force. At least...that's what she claims. Honestly, it just looks like she's squishing them with her giant foot.<br />
Also, it's the fastest of all Noble Phantasms.</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph paragraph--type--servant-profile-entry paragraph--view-mode--default">
<h3 class="sub-title" style="margin-top:8px">Profile 5</h3>
<p>In actuality, the legend of Paul Bunyan was seldom told during the pioneering days.</p>
<p>The tales of Bunyan's extraordinary feats commonly known today were mostly fabricated during the 20th century. Bunyan's folkloric heroism is shallow, and in name only, which may explain why she is unable to demonstrate such power as a Servant.</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph paragraph--type--servant-profile-entry paragraph--view-mode--default">
<h3 class="sub-title" style="margin-top:8px">Profile 6</h3>
<p><em>Clear Learning with Manga: All the Statesmen Event</em></p>
<p>Paul Bunyan may be mere legend, but her delightful tales are still passed down in present times...known by a great many, even a hundred years later.</p>
<p>Even if they aren't historically accurate, even if they are utter nonsense, legends passed down from person to person will be beloved for all time by people everywhere.</p>
<p>Hopefully she understands that.</p>
</div>

  function getRank(array, stat){
    var rank = -1;
    var count = 0;
    for(var i = 0; i < array.length; i++){
      if(array[i] == stat){
        rank = i+1;
        return rank;
      }
    }
  }
  function getInfo(array, field){
    var uniques = [];
    var highest = -1;
    for(var i = 0; i < array.length; i++){
      var currVal = array[i][field];
      var currValInt = parseInt(currVal);
      var currClass = array[i].tdoll_class;
      if(currValInt > highest){
        highest = currValInt;
      }
      uniques.push(currVal);
    }
    return [uniques, highest];
  }

function sortJSONByField(json, field){
  return json.concat().sort(function(a, b){
    return parseInt(b[field]) - parseInt(a[field]);
  });
}
