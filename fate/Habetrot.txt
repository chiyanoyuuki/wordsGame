<h2 class="main-title">Overview</h2>
<p><span><span><span><span><em><span>“Brides are splendid! Aren’t they just dazzling and soft, like a beautiful flower, or a radiant bird? There’s no reason not to like ‘em!”</span></em></span></span></span></span></p>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>This Servant profile is supposed to talk about Habetrot, but she can be a little hard to see. Just look down, and you’ll see her flying around in her airplane, ready to bust the enemy open as an </span></span></span></span></span></span><span><span><span><strong><span><span>AoE Arts Damage Dealer / Sacrifice Servant. </span></span></strong></span></span></span><span><span><span><span><span><span>But it’s not to worry, she’ll still let you summon her for free!</span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>Habetrot has two main uses. The first is as a looping Arts Servant. With her massive charge and Arts boost on </span></span></span></span></span></span><span><span><span><strong><span><span>Quick Spinning B</span></span></strong></span></span></span><span><span><span><span><span><span>, she can easily fill her NP bar and loop against most classes when supported with double Castoria. The second use she has is as a fire-and-die Servant, capable of unleashing her Noble Phantasm and then killing herself in favor of another Servant, thanks to her </span></span></span></span></span></span><span><span><span><strong><span><span>Protector of Brides EX</span></span></strong></span></span></span><span><span><span><span><span><span> skill. She’s also a fairly good support Servant for female allies, capable of healing them with </span></span></span></span></span></span><span><span><span><strong><span><span>Weaving Thread of Fortune A</span></span></strong></span></span></span><span><span><span><span><span><span> and offering them further heals per turn and invincibility with Protector of Brides.</span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>This little fae does have a few issues. For one, she’s on the lower side of damage, being freely available and only having one skill’s worth of damage boosts. For another, she’s rather squishy and will be hard to keep on the field if she’s hit. It should also be mentioned that it takes a good amount of time and effort to summon enough copies of her, so she may be locked out to Masters without significant amounts of Friend Points and time.</span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>Despite her size, Habetrot makes sure to be a versatile component of any Master’s team, serving in multiple potential capacities. Just don’t be mean to brides around her!</span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
<h2 class="main-title">Strengths</h2>
<div id="servant-strengths">
<h3><span><span><span><span><span><span>AoE Arts Looping</span></span></span></span></span></span></h3>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>Habetrot is among the premiere Arts loopers thanks to her 80% charge. This allows her to potentially loop even against nodes without 3 enemies per wave while using a Black Grail, dealing significant damage. In a double Castoria and Oberon setup, she can potentially loop on 1-1-x type nodes depending on if she can refund 30% on either of the first two waves. </span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
<h3><span><span><span><span><span><span>Dying</span></span></span></span></span></span></h3>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>Habetrot’s third skill allows her to die after any turn. This can allow her to serve as a Servant similar to Arash, charging up herself, clearing a wave, and then getting out of the way. Thanks to her high charge, she is capable of using a Black Grail and dying, unlike Arash, as she can have her Append 2 skill at max and use the 80% charge. Even without it, using her to clear a wave allows other Servants to use a Black Grail when they otherwise would not be able to due to charging issues.</span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
<h3><span><span><span><span><span><span>Support</span></span></span></span></span></span></h3>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>Habetrot has all the benefits of a support Servant. To all Servants, she offers a general heal and debuff immunity. Further, she is willing to drop a good pool of stars off her Noble Phantasm for Servants in later waves, while gladly passing on the Stars herself with a Star Gather rate down buff on herself. Yet to Females she can offer even more, including Guts, Invincibility, and Healing per turn. She’s a huge support for her allies, especially in situations where she sacrifices herself.</span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
</div>
<h2 class="main-title">Weaknesses</h2>
<div id="servant-weaknesses">
<h3><span><span><span><span><span><span>Low Damage</span></span></span></span></span></span></h3>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>Habetrot only has her a small Arts damage boost on her second skill and a NP damage boost on her Noble Phantasm. Compared to other Rider loopers she does not offer anywhere near the damage. She does benefit from the Black Grail if she can use it, but so can many other Riders. It should be mentioned that in a setup with two Castorias and an Oberon she gains a ton of damage on Wave 3 as most of her damage boosts will be from NP Damage up.</span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
<h3><span><span><span><span><span><span>Low Durability</span></span></span></span></span></span></h3>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>Habetrot’s third skill kills her. That should be enough to talk about her durability, but there are other issues in her kit. Her HP is on the lower end of things, and she has no real hard damage resists. Even her third skill which offers invulnerability for a turn will prove to be useless as she’ll still kill herself and revive with minimal HP. To make matters worse, her second skill damages her upon use. She isn’t going to stay long on the field if she takes damage from enemies.</span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
<h3><span><span><span><span><span><span>Annoying Acquisition</span></span></span></span></span></span></h3>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>Habetrot is one of the few 4-star Servants available in the Friend Point summoning pool, and is only added there after finishing Chapter 6. Her summon rate is 0.05% estimated, meaning a Master will need to spend about 400,000 Friend points per copy. This is not easy to acquire outside of lotteries, so many Masters will be stuck with her at lower NP levels and no way to acquire more copies. Further, she really starts shining with her second append unlocked, which is only possible with at least 2 copies or incredibly high bond levels.</span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
<h3><span><span><span><span><span><span>Party Cost</span></span></span></span></span></span></h3>
<p><span><span><span><span><span><span>Unlike her most standard comparison in Arash, she costs a full 12 points to the party. Because of this, a party cannot have all four of Oberon, two mainline 5-star Caster buffers, full 5-star CE slotted, and a 5-star Servant available after her death.</span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
</div>

<h2 class="main-title">Profile</h2>
<div class="paragraph paragraph--type--servant-profile-entry paragraph--view-mode--default">
<h3 class="sub-title" style="margin-top:8px">Character Info</h3>
<p>Habetrot is a fairy from Scottish folklore, and is associated with the spinning of thread. She is said to spin thread into cloth using cotton flowers and flax fibers, and to make clothing from that cloth. She is typically depicted as a child-sized old woman with a crooked back. Her role is as a bridal fairy who aids women in preparing for marriage and tailors bridal gowns for the busy brides. Anyone would wish for the help she provides.</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph paragraph--type--servant-profile-entry paragraph--view-mode--default">
<h3 class="sub-title" style="margin-top:8px">Profile 1</h3>
<p>Height/Weight: 50–60cm, 10–20kg<br />
Origin: British folklore<br />
Region: Scotland<br />
Alignment: Neutral-Good<br />
Gender: Female<br />
"Brides are great! They're so soft and fluffy, and they light up the room! They're as pretty as flowers and as brilliant as birds! Who wouldn't like them!?"</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph paragraph--type--servant-profile-entry paragraph--view-mode--default">
<h3 class="sub-title" style="margin-top:8px">Profile 2</h3>
<p>Habetrot of Faerie Britain<br />
She makes her first appearance as the spinster faerie during a Fragment section of the story. She traveled alongside Aesc the Savior and helped to put an end to the many calamities plaguing Britain. When Mash Rayshifted to Fae Era 400 and met her, Habetrot's fate took a drastic turn...or rather, she did everything in her power to overcome her own destiny.</p>
<p>When Aesc and the rest of the first Tam Lin disappeared from history, Habetrot wandered across Britain alone, spreading wedding culture and attending to numerous brides. Once the Era of the High Queen began, she turned herself into stone inside the Twisted Hole in northern Britain, where she fell into a deep slumber until her fateful encounter in 2019. After casually saving Mash at Sheffield, she guided Chaldea's Master to Orkney where she finally accepted the consequences of her choices.</p>
<p>In the Lostbelt, this faerie's name was originally Totorot. When Mash taught her the legend of Habetrot from Proper Human History, she decided to call herself Habetrot after she awoke from her stone slumber, in respect for Habetrot's way of life.</p>
<p>　　◆</p>
<p>Totorot was originally something of a hooligan of the Wing Clan, and she joined Aesc in her travels only after Aesc defeated her in a duel. (Though born as a faerie of thread-spinning, there were no human brides to tend to in the Lostbelt, forcing her to live without pursuing her purpose. This suffering twisted her into the brash hooligan she became.) After that duel, she fought alongside Aesc as one of the first Tam Lin.</p>
<p>Additionally, the Fragment story mentions that the name "Mash" was written on Mash's shield in chalk; this was Habetrot's doing. When Habetrot asked to look at the shield, she surreptitiously wrote Mash's name on it. The fact that chalk is commonly used to measure dimensions on cloth also shows that it was Habetrot.</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph paragraph--type--servant-profile-entry paragraph--view-mode--default">
<h3 class="sub-title" style="margin-top:8px">Profile 3</h3>
<p>Personality<br />
Introverted, yet active, she may not be very assertive, but she is proactive about pursuing what she wants and needs. She likes good people and can't stand evil people. Her neutral alignment does allow her to distinguish between good and evil, and she refrains from making judgments about which is better. In short, Habetrot doesn't hate people.</p>
<p>Her values are: peace is "good because it's easy," while conflict is "not good because it's tiresome." She's pretty much happy as long as she can keep on doing her needlework.</p>
<p>The Lostbelt Habetrot did not have the cultural familiarity with brides needed to fulfill her purpose, which caused issues with her memory capacity, but the original Habetrot is very intelligent despite her small stature. One common element between the Lostbelt and Proper Human History Habetrot is her kindheartedness. Humans tend not to fare well in interactions with faeries, but Habetrot never swerves in her protection of brides. Even if the bride is a sinner or has a questionable past, Habetrot will go so far as to sacrifice herself if she thinks it will help the bride have a happy future.</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph paragraph--type--servant-profile-entry paragraph--view-mode--default">
<h3 class="sub-title" style="margin-top:8px">Profile 4</h3>
<p>[Spinster Habetrot]<br />
Rank: EX<br />
NP Type: Anti-Army<br />
Range: 10-99<br />
Maximum Targets: 30</p>
<p>Wheel of Time That Spins for You.<br />
Also known as the Habbycat Cannon.<br />
Habetrot's actual Noble Phantasm is called "Bridal Spinwheel," which is a dress that glorifies the bride, but for...reasons, it's become the gigantic cannon firing ludicrously large shells you see no. (The Habetrot summoned to Chaldea is from Proper Human History, and she has absolutely no clue why this weapon resides within the core of her soul.)</p>
<p>When Habetrot uses this Noble Phantasm, a flywheel serves as an alternative to a magical energy reactor core to generate magical energy, turning into a physical weapon that fires buttons like a railgun. It is not imbued with the concept of mortality.</p>
<p>　　◆</p>
<p>The spinning wheel Habetrot pilots stores a broken Black Barrel inside. (Habetrot hides Black Barrel under a fluffy cloth.) However, mere possession of a cutting-edge weapon like the Black Barrel is poison to a faerie. Despite that, she kept it in her care until the very end because she instinctively knew that Mash would eventually need it. And so Habetrot continued to safeguard the Black Barrel as a gift for Mash's―her friend's― future.</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph paragraph--type--servant-profile-entry paragraph--view-mode--default">
<h3 class="sub-title" style="margin-top:8px">Profile 5</h3>
<p>Habetrot the Faerie<br />
Whether she is the Habetrot of Proper Human History or the Lostbelt version, her essence remains the same. Whenever she sees a girl she feels is the one (someone who has the potential to become a happy bride), she focuses all her energy into helping and supporting them. Habetrot refers to these girls as her brides. Once she sends off the bride, she goes in search for another. Even should she already have a bride in her care, if she finds another girl, she will devote herself to both, which has led some to say that Habetrot is prone to falling in love. She may not say it out loud, but Habetrot is a bundle of devotion. She is a self-sacrificing faerie who puts the happiness of her brides before her own.</p>
<p>　　◆</p>
<p>Habetrot values brides so highly because she can never mature. As much as she would like to be a bride herself, that wish cannot be granted. So she at least tries to protect the futures of those girls she deems beautiful enough to fall in love at first sight. Her protection of brides can be seen to some extent as living vicariously through them, or perhaps a dazzling dream... </p>
<p>"Maybe I can be like those girls someday."</p>
</div>

  function getRank(array, stat){
    var rank = -1;
    var count = 0;
    for(var i = 0; i < array.length; i++){
      if(array[i] == stat){
        rank = i+1;
        return rank;
      }
    }
  }
  function getInfo(array, field){
    var uniques = [];
    var highest = -1;
    for(var i = 0; i < array.length; i++){
      var currVal = array[i][field];
      var currValInt = parseInt(currVal);
      var currClass = array[i].tdoll_class;
      if(currValInt > highest){
        highest = currValInt;
      }
      uniques.push(currVal);
    }
    return [uniques, highest];
  }

function sortJSONByField(json, field){
  return json.concat().sort(function(a, b){
    return parseInt(b[field]) - parseInt(a[field]);
  });
}
